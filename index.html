North-to-South Ireland Road Trip (Antrim → Waterford)

This itinerary starts on the Antrim Coast (Northern Ireland), then drops south through Rostrevor & Carlingford, the Boyne Valley, Dublin, Kildare & Dunamase, Kilkenny, and finishes in Waterford. It’s built for a group that loves great food, culture, and a bit of quirky “you’ll remember this forever” Ireland. Some of the greatest adventures you can have is to get out and drive along the coasts and nature routes

-

Two great websites to use, while exploring the area are:

DiscoverIreland.ie

DiscoverNorthernIreland.com

Tip: for the easiest pacing, consider 2-night bases (e.g., Antrim Coast / Rostrevor or Carlingford / Dublin / Kilkenny or Waterford).

Day 1 — The Antrim Coast (Giants, cliffs, rope bridge, GoT vibes)

Base idea: Portrush / Portstewart / Ballycastle TV/Film + Epic Coast

Core plan

  • Giant’s Causeway — the iconic basalt columns and sea walk. Official site: National Trust
  • Carrick-a-Rede Rope Bridge — short, punchy adrenaline + amazing views. Info: National Trust
  • Dark Hedges — famous as a Game of Thrones location. Go early/late for atmosphere. Tourism info: Discover NI
  • Ballintoy Harbour — rugged little harbour used for Game of Thrones scenes. Discover NI: Discover NI
>

Food note: this coast is strong for seafood and cosy pubs. Keep driving legs short; pick 2–3 “big stops” and let the scenery do the work.

Optional add-ons (choose one)

  • Dunluce Castle (dramatic clifftop ruins). Discover NI: link
  • Causeway Coastal Route viewpoints — park, short walks, photos, repeat.
  • GoT Studio Tour (Banbridge) — if the group wants a dedicated TV/film experience. Official: Game of Thrones Studio Tour

Day 2 — Belfast (optional) → Mourne Mountains → Rostrevor

Base idea: Rostrevor (quiet, scenic) / Newcastle (coastal) Scenery + Easy Walking

Two ways to run the day

  • Option A (City + Coast): Belfast morning — museums, murals, coffee, then drive south into the Mournes.
  • Option B (All nature): straight to Mournes — easy hikes, viewpoints, seaside strolls.

Mournes + Rostrevor highlights

  • Rostrevor Forest & Kilbroney Park — gentle trails and big views. Info: Discover NI
  • Newcastle — classic seaside promenade with Mournes backdrop.
  • “Narnia” vibe — the Mournes are widely linked to C.S. Lewis inspiration (great “storytelling landscape” feel).

Optional adrenaline near Newry (choose one)

  • Outdoor karting + paintball — Lyons Karting (outdoor karting + paintball packages; accessible from the wider Newry area)
  • Paintball / clay pigeon / multi-activity — Watchtower Adventures (near Jonesborough; hillside views)
  • Family-friendly “giant story trail” — Slieve Gullion Forest Park

If the group’s energy is mixed, do a scenic drive + one short walk, then a long dinner in Rostrevor/Newcastle.

Day 3 — Carlingford & the Cooley Peninsula (castles, sea air, food)

Base idea: Carlingford (walkable, great pubs) Food + Coastal Town

What to do

  • Carlingford Castle (King John’s Castle) — compact ruins with big history. Reference: Heritage Ireland
  • Town wander + oysters/seafood — Carlingford is a “slow afternoon” win: shops, coffee, then a long dinner.
  • Cooley Peninsula drive — viewpoints, beaches, and story-rich landscapes (the “Táin” country).

If your group likes “charming + comfortable”: Carlingford is one of the best “reasonable but feels special” bases on this route.

Optional add-ons

  • Short coastal walk — low effort, high reward views.
  • Craft + culture — look for local galleries/handmade goods in town.
  • Sunset on the lough — this is a “photos + memory” stop, not a checklist stop.

Day 4 — Boyne Valley: Newgrange + Hill of Tara + Battle of the Boyne + Drogheda “Shrunken Head”

Base idea: Dublin (or near Drogheda) Ancient Ireland + Quirky Stop

Core plan (the heavy-hitters)

  • Brú na Bóinne (Newgrange) — UNESCO-scale ancient site. Visitor info: OPW / Brú na Bóinne Visitor Centre
  • Hill of Tara — symbolic seat of ancient Irish kingship. Reference: Heritage Ireland
  • Battle of the Boyne — pivotal 1690 site and visitor centre. OPW: Battle of the Boyne Visitor Centre

Drogheda (quirky + memorable)

  • St Peter’s Church — the preserved head of St Oliver Plunkett (the “shrunken head” stop). Background: Atlas Obscura
  • It’s unusual, conversation-starting, and genuinely memorable for visitors who like the “strange corner of history” moments.

Practical: check opening times locally—church access can vary by services/events.

Day 5 — Dublin (Trinity, museums, Phoenix Park, Dublin Castle)

Base idea: Dublin city (central) Museums + Iconic Sights

Classic “first-time in Dublin” loop

  • Trinity College & the Book of Kells — Ireland’s best-known manuscript experience. Official: Trinity / Book of Kells
  • National Museum of Ireland (Archaeology) — gold, bog bodies, and deep time. Official: museum.ie
  • Dublin Castle — layers of power/history in the centre. Official: Heritage Ireland
  • Phoenix Park — massive urban park; easy walking and breathing space. Info: Phoenix Park
  • Dublin Zoo (inside Phoenix Park) — great if the group wants a lighter, feel-good afternoon. Official: Dublin Zoo

Optional coastal “posh villages” add-on

  • Howth — cliff walk + seafood. Tourism: Visit Dublin
  • Dalkey — charming village feel, sea air, nice dinner. Tourism: Visit Dublin

If they’re museum-tired: do Phoenix Park + Howth/Dalkey instead of more “indoors.”

Day 6 — Kildare Village + Irish National Stud & Japanese Gardens + Rock of Dunamase

Base idea: Dublin or Kildare area Shopping + Gardens + Ruins

Kildare (easy, polished day)

  • Kildare Village — designer outlet browsing in a tidy setting. Official: kildarevillage.com
  • Irish National Stud & Japanese Gardens — calm, beautiful, very visitor-friendly. Official: irishnationalstud.ie

This day is deliberately “lighter” after Dublin.

Rock of Dunamase (Strongbow connection)

  • Rock of Dunamase — dramatic hilltop ruins with big views; great for photos and story-telling. OPW: Heritage Ireland
  • This is the kind of place that feels “ancient Ireland” without needing a long time onsite.

Day 7 — Kilkenny (medieval city energy + castle)

Base idea: Kilkenny city (walkable) Medieval Streets + Food

Three “must do” Kilkenny experiences

  • Kilkenny Castle — signature site + riverside parklands. OPW: Heritage Ireland
  • Medieval Mile — short distances, stacked history (churches, lanes, museums). Official: Kilkenny Tourism
  • Smithwick’s Experience — if the group enjoys a brewery story + tasting. Official: smithwicksexperience.com

Kilkenny is one of Ireland’s easiest “everyone’s happy” towns: compact, lively, good restaurants, no heavy driving once you arrive.

Optional add-ons

  • St Canice’s Cathedral & Round Tower — climb for views if knees/energy allow. Info: stcanicescathedral.ie
  • Evening: music + food — Kilkenny shines after 7pm.

Optional Day (swap-in after Kilkenny) — Wicklow: Glendalough + Guinness Lake + Powerscourt

Use as: add-on day, or swap with a “rest day” Vikings filming + Valleys

Wicklow “river valleys + monastic Ireland”

  • Glendalough — the monastic valley: round tower, lakes, forest walks. Wicklow Mountains NP info: nationalparks.ie
  • Guinness Lake (Lough Tay) — iconic viewpoint and dark-water lake.
  • TV/film note: the Wicklow area has been used for screen work; at Lough Tay there’s even a documented “Kattegat” reference tied to the Vikings TV series in Wicklow imagery. (See: Wikimedia Lough Tay category)

Powerscourt (gardens + waterfall)

  • Powerscourt Gardens — 47 acres of formal terraces and views. Official: Powerscourt Gardens
  • Powerscourt Waterfall — Ireland’s highest waterfall (121m) and an easy nature stop. Official: Powerscourt Waterfall

If you do Wicklow, it works best as a “fresh air reset” between city/history days.

Day 8 — Waterford (Vikings + medieval + crystal)

Base idea: Waterford city Viking City + Craft

Three strong Waterford picks

  • Waterford Treasures museums — the best way to get the city’s story quickly. Official: waterfordtreasures.com
  • Reginald’s Tower — Waterford’s Viking-age fortification and a great “start here” point. Ref: Waterford Treasures
  • Waterford Treasures museums — the best way to get the city’s story quickly. Official: waterfordtreasures.com
  • House of Waterford Crystal — factory tour is a crowd-pleaser (craft + spectacle). Official: waterford.com

Optional add-ons

  • Viking Triangle — walkable cluster of heritage sites in the city centre. Info: Discover Ireland
  • Seaside extension — if the group wants fresh air after museums, consider a short coastal spin.
